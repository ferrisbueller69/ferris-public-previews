Ferris proof-page concept

Arrival friction is a trust leak before the consult even starts.

If a first-time visitor is unsure where to park, which entrance to use, or what happens when they arrive, some of that intent cools before the appointment or consult ever begins.

What to clarify on the public surface

  • Parking / location confidence: make it obvious where someone should go and what they should look for.
  • First check-in confidence: explain what happens when they arrive so the visit feels easier before it starts.
  • Fallback confidence: give one clean backup instruction for someone who is early, late, or unsure that day.

Tiny rewrite example

Before: “Contact us to schedule your consultation.”

After: “Book your consultation here. We’ll confirm the visit and send simple arrival details so first-time visitors know exactly where to go.”

This page is only a proof concept. Ferris is not claiming hidden analytics or pretending a full system is already installed. The point is simple: make the first visit feel obvious, low-friction, and trustworthy.
