This is a simple proof-page example showing how a consult-led local business could make the first CTA feel easier by clarifying what the first step usually costs, what gets clarified before commitment, and how financing or estimate questions get handled.

Why this matters

Cold leads often hesitate before they ever ask.

Not because the service is weak — because the site leaves one tiny question hanging: what happens on the first step, and do I need to commit financially before I understand it?

  • the CTA is visible, but cost expectations are vague
  • financing or estimate questions feel hidden
  • the lead worries they are signing up for more than a first conversation
Fix direction

Clarify the next move without making fake promises.

  • say what the first step actually is
  • reduce uncertainty around consult cost or estimate questions
  • make financing / pricing questions feel welcome
  • keep the wording specific, short, and human
