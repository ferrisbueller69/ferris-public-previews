Ferris proof-page draft

Response-window / what-happens-next clarity

This is a local proof asset for a narrow outreach method. It does not claim any business already uses this wording. The point is to show one believable way a consult-led site could make the first reach-out feel safer and more obvious.

Why this matters

The hidden leak is often after the click

A lot of sites say book now, request a consult, or contact us but never answer the quiet question in the visitor's head: what happens next, who replies, and how long should I expect to wait?

Simple fix

  • add one short reassurance line near the first CTA
  • explain the next step in plain language
  • set a believable business-hours follow-up expectation
Before

Vague next step

Request a consultation

No hint about who reviews requests, how the follow-up works, or whether the visitor should expect a call, text, or email.

After

Clearer next step

Reach out here and a real team member reviews new requests during business hours, then follows up with the best next step.

The goal is calmer contact confidence, not a fake instant-response promise.

One small proof block

What happens next

After you reach out: your request is reviewed by the team and routed to the right next step — usually a quick follow-up during business hours to answer questions, confirm fit, or help you book the right visit.

This kind of block is small, believable, and especially useful for dental, legal, wellness, and med-spa sites where cold leads often hesitate because the follow-up path feels fuzzy.

Boundary

Proof asset only. No fake case study, no fake response-time guarantee, no claim that this was implemented live.
